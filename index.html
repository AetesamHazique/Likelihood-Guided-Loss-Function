Likelihood Guided Loss Functions for Image Restoration under Deep Learning Frameworks

Nitin Raj, Hazique Aetesam
Birla Institute of Technology Mesra, Patna Campus
Under Review
Code

Abstract

Modern imaging systems are frequently compromised by en- vironmental and sensor-induced noise, which diminishes visual fidelity and impairs critical downstream tasks such as classification, segmenta- tion, object detection; to name a few. While deep learning provides ro- bust denoising solutions, standard architectures typically rely on generic loss functions that disregard the underlying statistical properties of spe- cific degradations. To address this, we systematically investigate the in- tegration of noise-specific loss functions (derived from maximum likeli- hood estimator (MLE)) into three benchmark image restoration frame- works—REDNet, DnCNN, and FFDNet—across Gaussian, Poisson, Ran- dom Valued Impulse Noise (RVIN), and Multiplicative Gamma noise profiles. Experimental results demonstrate that tailoring the objective function to the distinct noise distribution consistently yields superior vi- sual quality and quantitative metrics compared to conventional, generic training strategies. By aligning the loss function with the statistical na- ture of the noise, this approach enables highly reliable and adaptive im- age restoration, offering significant potential for applications in remote sensing, medical imaging, low-light photography, and industrial inspec- tion.
